Mario Kart Features

Racing

Player gets to pick a Mario Kart character and a bike or cart to compete in a race with 11 other players. There are 32 possible tracks to race on, each having to do with a different setting/character in Mario Kart. There are multiple racing modes such as the Grand Prix, Time Trials, and Versus.

Coin Runners/ Balloon Battles

A time based minigame in Mario Kart where the player needs to collect as many coins or keep safe as many balloons as possible and prevent the enemy from collecting more coins/ balloons. All the regular rules and power up boxes are available, but the player is not racing.

Mario Kart 8 Deluxe:

Mario Kart 8 Deluxe released in 2017 is the newest version of Mario Kart. It includes features like anti-gravity (players can drive up ceilings and walls), three new characters, and 16 new tracks. It also comes with five different battle modes.

Battle Modes:

  • Balloon Battle: Classic game where players use power-ups to pop opponents balloons and keep their owns safe to score points
  • Renegade Roundup: Racers are divided into two teams, Renegades and Authorities, and need to capture players form the other team. Players can free their teammates by driving over a key switch placed under the jail cell. The Renegades win if they have at least one member free. The Authorities win if all the Renegade are captured.
  • Bob-omb Blast: A game from Mario Kart:Double Dash!!. Players attack each other using Bob-ombs to score points. Similar to Balloon Battle, but the only items are Bob-ombs
  • Coin Runners: Classic game where players collect coins scattered across the course to win points.
  • Shine Thief: A game from Mario Kart:Double Dash!!, players must retrieve a Shine Sprite and hold it as the opponents try to force the player to drop it. The team must collectively keep the Shine Sprite between them for 30 seconds to win.
